No child is property. Cruelty is not a right.

Abuse, harassment, bullying, and exploitation are not discipline. They are violations. Indian law recognizes your right to safety, education, and dignity.

POCSO Act, 2012

Protects children under 18 from sexual abuse, exploitation, and harassment. Covers physical contact, non-contact offenses, and digital exploitation. Reports can be made anonymously to authorities or CHILDLINE.

Juvenile Justice Act, 2015

Ensures child-friendly legal procedures, care, and protection for minors in distress or conflict with the law. Mandates specialized courts and rehabilitation over punishment for children.

RTE Act, 2009

Guarantees free and compulsory education for ages 6–14. No child can be denied schooling due to poverty, caste, gender, disability, or displacement. Schools must provide safe environments.

⚖️ Note: NSCI provides educational awareness only. This is not legal advice. Consult licensed professionals for case-specific guidance.

Your safety comes first. Here is how to protect yourself.

1
Tell someone you trust: A teacher, relative, counselor, or neighbor who will listen without judgment or blame.
2
Save evidence safely: Keep messages, screenshots, photos, or records in a secure place. Do not delete them, even if asked.
3
Contact official helplines: Trained professionals are available 24/7. CHILDLINE 1098 is free, confidential, and child-focused.
4
Avoid isolation: Stay in safe, visible spaces. Do not meet strangers alone or share personal details online.
5
Seek emotional support: Counseling and peer support groups exist to help you recover at your own pace. You are not broken.

Disclaimer & Legal Boundaries

National ShieldChild India (NSCI) is an independent, unregistered awareness and advocacy initiative. We are not a government body, law enforcement agency, legal firm, or medical provider. We cannot guarantee legal outcomes, intervene in investigations, or replace official authorities. All content on this platform is educational and intended for public awareness. In emergencies, always contact local police, official helplines, or licensed professionals immediately.

Privacy Commitment

We do not sell, share, or trade user data. Information voluntarily shared remains confidential to the extent permitted by Indian law. In cases involving imminent threat to life or mandatory reporting obligations, we may cooperate with authorized authorities. We do not collect unnecessary personal data. Parents and guardians are encouraged to supervise minors’ online activity.

Terms of Use

  • Do not submit false, defamatory, or unverified accusations.
  • Do not harass, threaten, or impersonate others.
  • Do not upload illegal, explicit, or harmful material.
  • NSCI reserves the right to remove content that violates safety guidelines or Indian law.

Reporting & Safety Policy

  • We listen without judgment and provide educational guidance only.
  • We never promise absolute secrecy. Mandatory reporting laws may require official intervention.
  • We never advise running away, confronting alleged abusers, or taking vigilante action.
  • We encourage preserving evidence, contacting trusted adults, and using official helplines.
